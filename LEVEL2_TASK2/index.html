About Sachin



Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar, widely known as "The God of Cricket," is a former Indian cricketer and one of the most iconic sports figures in the world. He was born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India, and grew up in a middle-class family.


From a young age, Sachin showed a keen interest in cricket and began playing the sport at the age of 5. He was encouraged and supported by his elder brother, Ajit Tendulkar, who recognized his talent and enrolled him in a local cricket academy. Sachin's early years were filled with a deep passion for cricket, and he spent countless hours practicing and honing his skills.


At the age of 11 , Sachin's talent was noticed by the famous cricket coach Ramakant Achrekar, who became his mentor. Achrekar recognized Sachin's immense potential and provided him with rigorous training. Sachin's commitment, determination, and hard work paid off, and he quickly rose through the ranks of Mumbai's cricket circuit.


Sachin made his debut in Test cricket against Pakistan in November 1989 at the age of 16 . He quickly gained attention for his batting prowess and soon became a regular member of the Indian Test team. He played 200 Test matches, scoring 15,921 runs at an average of 53.78, including 51 centuries and 68 half-centuries. Sachin's performances in Test cricket were characterized by his exceptional technique, ability to play under pressure, and versatility in adapting to different conditions and opposition bowlers.


Sachin's ODI debut came against Pakistan in December 1989, and he went on to play 463 ODIs for India. He scored 18,426 runs at an average of 44.83, including 49 centuries and 96 half-centuries. Sachin's ODI career was marked by his aggressive batting style, which made him one of the most feared batsmen in the world. He was known for his ability to dominate bowling attacks and his consistent performances in high-pressure situations, making him a vital cog in the Indian ODI team.



Career Highlights


  • Awarded with Bharat Ratna highest civilian award
  • Awarded with the Padma Shri
  • Awarded with the Padma Vibhushan
  • Played 200 Test matches
  • Played 463 One Day Internationals
  • Highest run-scorer in Test and ODI cricket, with total of 34,357 runs.
  • Holds the record for the most centuries in international cricket 100 centuries
  • First batsman to score a double century in a One Day International
  • Retired from all forms of international cricket (2013)
